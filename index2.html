The Sites of Memory

Sites of memory are, more than anything else, places with high symbolic value for specific communities. In Colombia, a site of memory usually triggers images of violence tied to what different political and social actors call the Colombian civil war, the Colombian inner armed conflict, or the Colombian war against terror. Therefore, the Colombian sites of memory are highly relevant elements to understand Colombian politics and its relationship with violence.

The Colombian sites of memory constitute infrastructures of memory with the ability to facilitate or impede the circulation of past experience. In other words, those physical places help to reinforce particular interpretations of the past while they reject others. The map I am showing is the first attempt to portray sites of memory with conflicting narratives of the past in the same place.
